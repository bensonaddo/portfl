Welcome to Benson's Life Where It All Begins

My journey stated 8yrs ago as a QA Tester where I was tasked with manually testing out softwares based on a given requirement.

The first step in a manual QA tester's day typically involves reviewing the test plan. They collaborate with the development team to understand the requirements and objectives of the software under test. Based on this information, they create detailed test cases and test scripts, outlining step-by-step instructions to be executed during testing. This process requires meticulous attention to detail and a comprehensive understanding of the software's functionality

Once the test cases are ready, the manual QA tester proceeds with test execution. They carefully follow the predefined steps, performing functional, usability, performance, and regression tests. By recording observations, they identify defects, inconsistencies, or deviations from the expected behavior. These issues are then documented in bug reports, including detailed descriptions, steps to reproduce, and severity assessments. Clear and concise bug reports are crucial for the development team to understand and address the problems efficiently.
